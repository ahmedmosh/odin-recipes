Brunswick Stew

Description

This Brunswick stew is an old family recipe that's brimming with pork, beef, and chicken to make the best stew you've ever had!
Those of you who've never had Brunswick stew will hopefully love it.
If you've had it before, you won't believe how good this version tastes.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup chopped onions
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 1 ½ pounds ground pork
  • 1 ½ pounds ground beef
  • ½ cup hickory flavored barbeque sauce
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon hot sauce to taste
  • 1 green bell pepper
  • 3 (14.75 ounce) cans cream style corn

Steps

  1. Step 1

    Heat olive oil in a large skillet.
    Add onions and celery; cook and stir until soft. Mix in pork and beef;
    cook until evenly browned and crumbly. Do not drain.

  2. Step 2

    STransfer pork and beef mixture to a large stock pot over low heat. Stir in shredded chicken, tomatoes and their liquid, ketchup, and barbeque sauce.
    Season with salt, pepper, and hot sauce. Place the whole green pepper into the mixture. Cook, stirring occasionally, until thickened, about 2 hours.

  3. Step 3

    Stir the cream-style corn into the stew mixture;
    continue cooking for about 1 hour, or until desired consistency.
    Remove green pepper; chop and return to the stew or discard.
